## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Purpose**: Establish independent ethics oversight for YUTHUB to ensure:
1. Algorithmic systems serve residents, not surveillance
2. Vulnerable population data protected to highest standards
3. Technology enhances, not replaces, human relationships
4. Power imbalances between staff and residents mitigated

**Key Principle**: **"Technology must be accountable to those it affects most"**

**Composition**: Majority care-experienced voices + multidisciplinary experts

---

## 1. MISSION & VALUES

### 1.1 Mission Statement

**To provide independent, expert oversight of YUTHUB's data practices, algorithmic systems, and technology design to ensure they uphold the rights, dignity, and autonomy of young people experiencing housing instability.**

### 1.2 Core Values

**1. Lived Experience Expertise**
- Care-experienced young people are the primary experts on what serves them
- Their voices carry equal weight to professional/technical expertise

**2. Transparency**
- All deliberations documented and (where appropriate) made public
- Decisions explained in plain language
- Conflicts of interest declared

**3. Accountability**
- Board has teeth: can block features, require changes
- Annual public report on platform ethics
- External audit of board effectiveness

**4. Intersectionality**
- Consider how technology affects people differently (race, disability, gender, etc.)
- Challenge one-size-fits-all approaches

**5. Precautionary Principle**
- If unsure about ethical implications: don't deploy until resolved
- "Move fast and break things" is incompatible with vulnerable populations

## 2. BOARD COMPOSITION

### 2.1 Membership Structure

**Total Members**: 9-11 (odd number for voting)

**Mandatory Seats**:
1-3. **Care-Experienced Young People** (3 seats)
   - Aged 18-30
   - Experience of supported housing between ages 16-21
   - At least 1 year since leaving supported housing
   - Diverse backgrounds (race, disability, geography)
   - **Paid**: ¬£1,000/year + ¬£200/meeting

4. **Safeguarding Expert**
   - Background in child/adult safeguarding
   - Experience with multi-agency working
   - Independent (not employed by YUTHUB clients)

5. **Data Protection / Privacy Expert**
   - Chartered member of IAPP or equivalent
   - Experience with GDPR, particularly vulnerable populations
   - Legal or technical background

6. **Data Scientist / AI Ethics Specialist**
   - Experience with algorithmic fairness, bias testing
   - Published work on ethical AI
   - Independent (not employed by YUTHUB)

7. **Youth Housing Sector Representative**
   - Senior leader from national charity (e.g., Centrepoint, St Basils)
   - 10+ years experience in sector
   - Strategic perspective on sector needs

8. **Clinician / Mental Health Professional**
   - Experience working with young people
   - Understands trauma-informed practice
   - Can assess psychological impact of platform features

9. **Legal Expert (Human Rights Focus)**
   - Barrister or solicitor with human rights background
   - Experience with Equality Act, Children Act, Care Act
   - Pro bono or paid nominal fee

**Optional Seats** (2 rotating positions, 12-month terms):
10. **Academic Researcher** (housing, social work, or HCI)
11. **Service User Representative** (from current YUTHUB pilot sites)

**Ex-Officio** (non-voting):
- YUTHUB CEO (or delegate)
- YUTHUB CTO (or delegate)
- YUTHUB Data Protection Officer

### 2.2 Recruitment & Selection

**Care-Experienced Members**:
- Open call via youth charities, care leaver networks
- Application form (not CV - barriers to access)
- Video interview option (not just written)
- Selection panel includes current care-experienced members (after Year 1)

**Professional Members**:
- Open recruitment with job description
- Application + interview
- Selection by existing board + YUTHUB leadership (50/50 vote)

**Criteria**:
- Commitment to mission and values
- Ability to attend 4 meetings/year
- No conflicts of interest (see Section 2.4)
- Diverse perspectives (avoid groupthink)

### 2.3 Term Limits

- **Care-Experienced Members**: 2 years (renewable once = max 4 years)
- **Professional Members**: 3 years (renewable once = max 6 years)
- **Staggered terms**: Avoid entire board turning over simultaneously

## 3. REMIT & POWERS

### 3.1 What the Board Reviews

**Mandatory Review** (before deployment):
1. **New Algorithmic Systems**
   - Risk scoring models
   - Predictive analytics
   - Automated alerts or flags
   - Any system making decisions about residents

2. **Data Collection Changes**
   - New data fields (especially special category data)
   - Changes to retention periods
   - New data sharing arrangements

3. **Features Affecting Resident Autonomy**
   - Surveillance features (location tracking, activity monitoring)
   - Restrictions on resident access to own data
   - Any "gamification" that may be manipulative

4. **Safeguarding Protocols**
   - Changes to incident reporting workflows
   - Escalation algorithms
   - Multi-agency data sharing

**Annual Review**:
- Algorithm performance (fairness audits)
- Data breach incidents and responses
- User complaints related to privacy/ethics
- Overall platform impact assessment

**Ad-Hoc Review** (on request):
- Any feature with ethical concerns raised by stakeholders
- Response to external criticism or regulatory action
- Major platform pivots or acquisitions

### 3.2 Powers & Decision Authority

**Advisory Powers** (recommendations to YUTHUB leadership):
- ‚úÖ Recommend design changes
- ‚úÖ Suggest alternative approaches
- ‚úÖ Propose new policies

**Veto Powers** (binding on YUTHUB):
- üõë **Block deployment** of features with unacceptable ethical risks
- üõë **Require fixes** before launch
- üõë **Demand suspension** of existing features pending review

**Veto Override**:
- YUTHUB leadership can override veto ONLY if:
  1. Legal obligation requires feature (e.g., safeguarding law)
  2. 75% of board agrees veto was inappropriate (rare)
  3. Override documented and published in annual report

**Escalation**:
- If board and YUTHUB cannot resolve disagreement:
  - External mediation (mutually agreed mediator)
  - Public statement by board (last resort)
  - Board resignation (nuclear option)

## 4. MEETING STRUCTURE

### 4.1 Frequency & Format

**Quarterly Meetings** (4/year):
- 3 hours duration
- In-person or hybrid (accommodate care-experienced members)
- Accessible location (or online)
- Refreshments provided

**Emergency Meetings**:
- Called by Chair or 3+ members
- 5 working days notice minimum
- For urgent ethical issues only

### 4.2 Typical Agenda

1. **Welcome & Apologies** (5 mins)
2. **Declarations of Interest** (5 mins)
3. **Minutes of Previous Meeting** (10 mins)
4. **Matters Arising** (10 mins)
5. **Standing Items**:
   - Platform metrics review (20 mins)
   - Recent complaints/incidents (20 mins)
6. **New Feature Reviews** (60 mins)
   - Presentations by YUTHUB team
   - Questions from board
   - Deliberation
7. **Policy Discussions** (30 mins)
8. **Annual Report Planning** (10 mins, Q4 only)
9. **Any Other Business** (10 mins)

### 4.3 Decision-Making

**Quorum**: 6 members (including at least 2 care-experienced members)

**Voting**:
- Simple majority for recommendations
- 2/3 majority (supermajority) for veto
- Care-experienced members have equal vote (no "junior" status)

**Consensus Preference**:
- Strive for consensus where possible
- If dissent: record minority opinion in minutes

## 7. CASE STUDIES (HYPOTHETICAL)

### Case Study 1: Location Tracking Feature

**Proposal**: YUTHUB wants to add GPS tracking for "safeguarding" (know where residents are)

**Board Review**:
- **Care-Experienced Members**: "Feels like being spied on. Would make me feel distrusted."
- **Safeguarding Expert**: "Could be useful in missing person cases, but very intrusive."
- **Legal Expert**: "Likely disproportionate interference with Article 8 (privacy rights)."

**Decision**: **VETO** - Feature blocked unless:
1. Resident explicitly opts in (not default)
2. Only activated in emergency (e.g., missing person report)
3. Resident can disable at any time
4. Data automatically deleted after 24 hours

**Outcome**: YUTHUB redesigned feature with opt-in model. Board approved revised version.

---

### Case Study 2: Predictive Eviction Risk

**Proposal**: Algorithm flags residents "at risk of eviction" based on rent arrears, incidents, etc.

**Board Review**:
- **Data Scientist**: "What's the accuracy? Risk of false positives stigmatizing residents."
- **Housing Sector Rep**: "Could be helpful for early intervention if used sensitively."
- **Care-Experienced Members**: "Being flagged 'at risk' could become self-fulfilling prophecy."

**Decision**: **CONDITIONAL APPROVAL** with safeguards:
1. Flag only visible to assigned key worker (not managers/directors)
2. Triggers supportive conversation, not punitive action
3. Resident must be told if flagged (transparency)
4. Algorithm audited for bias (ethnicity, disability)
5. 6-month trial with resident feedback

**Outcome**: Implemented with safeguards. 6-month review found positive impact (early support) with no negative effects. Continued.

## 8. DISSOLUTION & COMPLAINTS

### 8.1 Board Dissolution

**Board can be dissolved if**:
- YUTHUB ceases operations
- 75% of board votes to dissolve (must publish reasons)
- External audit finds board ineffective AND no improvements made

**Cannot be dissolved**:
- By YUTHUB leadership unilaterally
- To avoid accountability for controversial decision

### 8.2 Complaints About Board

**Internal Process**:
1. Complaint to Board Chair (in writing)
2. Chair investigates or appoints investigator
3. Response within 21 days
4. Appeal to full board if unsatisfied

**External Escalation**:
- Complaint to ICO (data protection issues)
- Complaint to sector regulator (housing quality)
- Media/public accountability (last resort)
